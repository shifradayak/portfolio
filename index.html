Hi, I'm Shifra!

I'm a journalist who loves telling people-centered stories through data, graphics, writing and more. My interests lie in reporting on policy and environmental issues.

This fall, I'll join the Allbritton Journalism Institute's second fellowship class, where I'll cover all things federal government for NOTUS. Right now, at the Connecticut Mirror, I'm telling data-driven stories about concentrated poverty, mental health, transportation and other Connecticut policy issues.

In May 2024, I graduated from the University of Maryland with dual degrees in journalism and environmental science and policy. While there, I served as news editor at my school's independent student publication, The Diamondback, and worked for Capital News Service and the Howard Center for Investigative Journalism.

I was born and raised in the Washington, D.C. area. When I'm not reporting, I enjoy reading, hiking and trying new recipes. Feel free to reach out and chat!
